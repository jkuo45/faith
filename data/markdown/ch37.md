1. Vaunt not (boast) about your body; because it was first formed. Nor of thy brain 🧠 because therein resides the soul. Is not the master of the house more honorable than its walls?
2. The ground must be prepared before corn can be planted. The potter must build his furnace before he can make his porcelain (luxury).
3. As the breath of Heaven says to the waters of the deep;
    
    > This way shall thy billions roll, and no other; thus high, and no higher shall they raise their fury.”
    > 
    
    So let thy spirit, O man, activate[?] and direct thy flesh; 
    So let it repress it’s wilderness.
    
4. [n/a]
5. Thy body is as the  globe 🌎 of the earth. Thy bones 🦴 the pillars that sustain it on its basis.
6. As the ocean 🌊 gives rise to springs ⛲️, whose waters return against into its bosom/heart ❤️ through the rivers, so runneth thy life from thy outwards, and so runneth it into its place again.
7. Do not both run their course forever ♾️ [water 💧, blood🩸]? Behold, the same God ordained them.
8. Is not thy nose 👃the channel to perfumes , thy mouth 👄 the path to delicacies 🍣.
9. Are not thine eyes 👀 the sentinels that watch for thee 🪽. Yet how often are they unable to distinguish truth from error?
10. Keep your soul in moderation. Teach thy spirit to be attentive to its good. So shall these, its ministers[?] always convey the truth.
11. Thine hand 🖐️, is it not a miracle? Is there anything like it in all of creation? Why was it given to us? But those mightiest stretch it out to the assistance of thy brother.
12. Why of all things living are we the only ones capable of blushing? The world shall read thy shame upon thy face; therefore do nothing shameful. 
13. Fear and dismay, which robs thy countenance of its ruddy splendor, avoid guilt, and thou shalt know that fear is beneath thee, that dismay is unamely.
