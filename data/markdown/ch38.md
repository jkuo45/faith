# Chapter 38: The Soul of Man

---

[https://hermetic.com/moorish/7koran#chapter-xxxviii](https://hermetic.com/moorish/7koran#chapter-xxxviii)

1. The blessing, O man, of thy external part is health, vigor, and proportion. The greatest of these is health. What health is to the body, even that is honesty to the soul.
2. That thou has a soul is of all knowledge, the most certain of all truths the most plain unto thee. Be meek, be grateful for it. Seek not to know it perfectly. It is inscrutable.
3. Thinking, understanding, reasoning, willing, call not these the soul. They are its actions, but they are not its essence.
4. Raise it [soul] not too high, that thou be not despised. Do not be like those who fall by climbing. Neither debase it to the sense of brutes, nor be thou like to the horse and the mule, in whom there is no understanding.
5. Search it [soul] by its faculties, know it by its virtues. They are more in number than the hairs of thy head. The stars of heaven are not to be counted with them.

1. Know that as thy heart, so also thy soul is one (*unique[?], or heart, soul = one[?]*).
2. Does not the sun harden the clay? Does it not also soften the wax? As it is one sun that worketh both, even so it is one soul willeth contraries.
3. As the moon retains her nature, though darkness speaks itself before her face as a curtain; so the soul remains perfect even in the heart (bosom) of a fool. [?]
4. She [soul] is immortal; she [soul] is unchangeable; she is alike in all Health calleth her forth to show her loveliness, and application annointeth her with the oil of wisdom.
5. Although she [soul] shall live after thee, think not she was born before thee. She was created with thy flesh, and formed with thy brain [?].

1. Justice could not give her [soul] to thee exalted by virtues, nor mercy deliver her to thee deformed by vices, These must thine and thou must answer for them.
2. Suppose not death can shield thee from examination; think not corruption can hide thee from inquiry. He who formed thee knowest not.
3. Perceive not the rooster the hour of midnight? Exalteth he not his voice to tell the morning. Dogs, the footsteps of its master, The herb thy heals the wounded goat.
4. Envy not to these their senses, because quicker than thine own. Learn that the advantage lies not in possessing good things, but in knowing how to use them.
5. Hadst thou the ear of the stag, or were thine eyes as strong and piercing as the eagle, hound/smell, ape/taste, tortoise/feeling. Yet without reason what would they avail thee? Perish not all these like their kindred?
6. Has any of them the gift of speech? Can any say unto thee. “Therefore did I do?” [”Why did I do?”]

Chapter 38 *Soul of Man* v. 11-25

1. The lips of the wise are as the doors of a cabinet. No sooner are they opened but treasures are poured out before thee.
2. Like unto trees of gold arranged in beds of silver are wise sentences uttered in due season.
3. Can thou think too greatly of thy soul? Or can too much be said in its praise? It is in the image of Him who gave it.
4. Remember thou its dignity forever; forget not how great a talent is committed to thy change.
5. Whatsoever may do good, may also do harm. Beware that thou direct its course to virtue.
6. Think not that thou canst lose her [soul] in a crowd; suppose not that thou canst bury her in thy closet. Action is her delight, and she [soul] will not be withheld from it.
7. Her [soul] motion is perpetual; her attempts are universal; her agility is not to be suppressed. Is it at the uttermost part of the earth? She will have it. Is it beyond the regions of the stars? Yet will her eye discover it? Inquiry is her delight. As one who transverseth the burning sands, in search of water, so is the soul that thirsteth after knowledge.
8. Guard her [soul] for she [soul] is rash; restrain her for she is irregular. Correct her for she is outrageous. More supple is she than water, more flexible than wax, more yielding than air. Is there aught that can bind her?
9. As a sword in the hand of a mad man, even so is the soul to him who wanteth discretion.

Chapter 38 *The Soul of Man* v.17-25

1. The end of her [soul] search is truth. Her means to discover it are reason and experience. But are these not weak, uncertain, and fallacious? How then shall she attain unto it?
2. General opinion is no proof of the truth, for the generality of men are ignorant.
3. Perception of thyself, the knowledge of Him who created thee, the sense of worship thou owest unto Him. Are not these plain before thy face? And behold! What is there more than man needeth to know.
