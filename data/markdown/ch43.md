## Verse 1-6

1. If there is anything lovely, if there is anything desirable, if there is anything within the reach of man that is worthy of praise, is it not knowledge? And yet who is it that attains it?
2. The statesman proclaims that he has it. The ruler of the people claim the praise of it - but find the subject that he possess it?

*[jk: do you find the subject that possess it?]*
3. Evil is not requisite to man; neither can vice be necessary to be tolerated. Yet how many evils are permitted by the connivance of the laws? How many crimes committed by the decree of the council！
4. But be wise, O ruler and learn, O thou that are to command the nations! One crime authorized by thee is worse than the escape of ten 🔟 from punishment.
5. When the poor are numerous, when thy sons increase about the table. Do not send them to slay the innocent, and to fall before the sword of him whom they have not offended.
6. If the objects of thy desire demanding the lives of a thousand sayeth thou not: “I will have it”. Surely from thou forgetteth that He Who created thee, created also these; and that their blood is as rich as thine.

## Verse 7-18

1. Sayeth thou, that justice cannot be executed without wrong? Surely thine own words condemn thee.
2. Thou who flattereth with false hopes the criminal that he may confess his guilt, art not thou unto him a criminal? Or art guiltless, because he cannot punish it?
3. When thou commandest to the torture him who is but suspected of ill, dareth thou to remember, that thou mayest rack the innocent?

*[jk: rack the innocent?]*
4. Is thy purpose answered by the event?
Is thy soul satisfied with his confession?

Pain will enforce him to say what is not; as easy as what is, and anguish hath caused innocence to accuse herself *[jk: ?]*.
5. That thou mayest not kill him without cause, thou does worse than kill him; that thou mayest prove if he be guilty, thou destroys his innocence.
6. O blindness to all truth! O insufficience of the wisdom of the wise! Know, when thy judge shall bid thee account for this, thou shalt wish ten thousand guilty to have gone free; rather than one innocent to stand forth against thee.
7. Insufficient as thou alt to the maintenance of justice how shalt thou arrive at the knowledge of truth? How shalt thou ascend to the footsteps of her throne ⚖️?
8. As the owl is blinded by the radiance of the sun, so shall the brightness of her [truth] countenance dazzle thee in thy approaches.
9. If thou wouldst mount onto her [truth] throne, first know thyself at her footstool 🦶🏼; if thou would arrive at the knowledge of her, first inform thyself of thine own ignorance 🫵🏻.
10. More worth is she than pearls ⚪️, therefore seek her carefully. The emerald 🟢 and the sapphire 🔵, and the ruby 🔴 are as dirt 🟤 beneath her feet; therefore pursue her *manfully [jk:❓]*.
11. The way to her is labor; attention is the pilot that must conduct three into her part. But weary not on the way. For when art arrived at her, the toil shall be to thee for pleasure.

[jk: pilot as in 🛩️, 🛥️?]
12. Say not unto thyself: 
    
    > Behold, truth breeds hatred, and i will avoid it. Dissimulation raises friends, and I will follow it.
    > 
    
    Are not the enemies made by truth, better than the friends obtained by flattery?

### Verse 19-28

1. Naturally does man desire the truth; yet when it is before him, he will not apprehend it. And if it forces itself upon him, he is not offended at it.
2. The fault is not in truth, for that is [amiable](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/amiable), but the weakness of man bares not its splendor.
3. Would we see our insufficiencies more plainly? View at thy devotions [jk: our idols]. To what end was religion instituted but to teach thee our infirmities (weaknesses), to show thee that from Heaven alone art thou to hope for good?
4. Does it [religion] not remind us that we are dust? Does it not tell us we are ashes? And behold repent once, is it not frailty [weak]?
5. When we give an oath, when we swear we will not deceive, behold it spreadeth shame upon they face, and upon the face of him that receives it. Learn to be just, and repentance may be forgotten. Learn to be honest and oaths won’t be necessary [jk:📝].
6. The shorter follies are, the better. ❌ Do not say to yourself:
    
    > I will not play the fool by halves.
    > 
7. He that has his own faults with patience, shall reprove another with boldness.
8. He that gives a denial with reason, shall suffer a repulse with moderation.
9. If thou art suspected, answer with freedom. Whom should suspicion affright (frighten), except the guilty?
10. The tender of heart is turned from his purpose by supplication. The proud is rendered more obstinate by entreaty; the sense of thine insufficiencies commandeth thee to hear; but to just, thou must hear without thy passions.

### supplication (gemma2:latest)

> `"Supplication" refers to the act of humbly or earnestly begging for something.`
> 

`Here's a breakdown:`

- **`It involves making a request:** Someone is asking for something they need or desire.`
- **`It emphasizes humility:** The person making the request recognizes their own powerlessness and relies on the other party's mercy or
generosity.`
- **`It often conveys urgency or desperation:** The supplicant may be pleading for help in a difficult situation.`

**`Examples:`**

- `"The prisoners made a supplication to the king for mercy."`
- `"She offered a supplication for rain during the drought."`
- `"His supplications fell on deaf ears."`
