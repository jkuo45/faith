# Chapter 33: Gratitude

---

[https://hermetic.com/moorish/7koran#chapter-xxxiii](https://hermetic.com/moorish/7koran#chapter-xxxiii)

1. As the branches of a tree return their sap to the root, from whence it arose, as a river pours its stream to the sea, when the spring was supplied; so the heart ♥️ of a grateful man delighteth in the returning of a benefit received.
2. He/She acknowledges their obligations with cheerfulness, looketh on his benefactor with love 🫶 and esteem 🤗.
3. And if to return it be not in his power, he nourishes the memory of it in his breast with kindness. He does not forget it not all the days of his life.
4. The heart of a grateful man is like the clouds of heaven which drops upon the earth fruits, herbage, and flowers, but the heart of the ungrateful is like a desert of sand which swallows, with greediness, the showers that fall, and burieth them in ints heart and produces nothing.
5. Envy not thy benefactor, neither strive to conceal the benefit he has conformed. To oblige is better than to be obliged (owe gratidude). Though the act of generosity commandeth admiration, yet the humility to touch the heart, and is amiable on the heart of God and man.
6. But receive not a favor from the hand of the proud. Do not have any obligations to the selfish or avaricious (greedy). The vanity of pride shall expose thee to shame. They [greedy] will never be satisfied.
