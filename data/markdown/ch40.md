# Chapter 40: The Instability of Man

---

[https://hermetic.com/moorish/7koran#chapter-xl](https://hermetic.com/moorish/7koran#chapter-xl)

1. Inconstancy (inconsistent, no pattern) is powerful in the heart of man; Intemperance sways us at will, Despair engrosseth us much of it; Fear takes ahold of us and proclaims  “Behold, I sit unrivaled therein.” but Vanity is beyond them all.
2. Weep not therefore at the calamities of the human slate, rather laugh at its fallacies. In the hands of the man addicted to vanity, life then is but then the shadow of a dream.
3. The hero, the most renowned of human character, what is he but the bubble 🫧 of this weakness. The public is unstable and ungrateful. Why should the man of wisdom endanger himself for fools.
4. The man who neglecteth his present concerns to revolve how he will behave when greater feedeth himself with wind, while his bread is eaten by another.
5. Act as becometh thee in thy present station, and in more exalted ones thy face shall not be ashamed.
6. Vanity blinds the eye and hides the heart of a man from himself. When you’re unable to see yourself, then others discover thee, most plainly.

1. As the tulip 🌷that is gaudy without smell, conspicuous without use, so is the man who sitteth himself up so high, and hath no merit.
2. The heart of the vain is troubled while it seems content. His cares are greater than his pleasures.
3. His solicitude cannot rest with his bones, the grave is not deep enough to hide it. He extends his thoughts beyond his being. He speaks praise to be paid when he is gone; whosoever promiseth it, deceiveth him.
4. ❓As the man who engageth his wife to remain in widowhood, that she disturb not his soul; so is he who expecteth that his praise shall reach his ears beneath the earth, or cherish his heart in its shroud.
5. Do well while you’re alive/live, but regard not what is said of it. Content thyself with deserving praise, and thy posterity shall rejoice in healing.
6. As the butterfly who seeth not his own colors, as the jasmine which feels not its own scent, so it the man who appears happy (gay), and biddeth others to take note of it.
7. To what purpose, if no eyes 👀 gaze upon them, if the world does not know? Give thy clothes to the naked, and thy food to the hungry; so shalt thou be praised, and feel that thou deserveth it.
8. Why bestoweth thou in every man the flattery of unmeaning words? One knows that once returns, it is disregarded. He knoweth he lieth unto thee, yet he knows he will receive thanks for it. Speak in sincerity and thou will hear with instruction 👂.
9. The vain delighteth to speak of himself, but he seeth not that others do not like to hear him 🤐.
10. If he has done anything worth praise or possesseth that which is worth of admiration, his joy is to proclaim it, his pride to hear it reported. The desire of such a man defeats himself.
11. The heart of man cannot attend to too many things at once. He who fixeth his soul on show (in public), loses reality. He pursues bubbles 🫧 which break in their flight, while he treads to earth what would him honor.
